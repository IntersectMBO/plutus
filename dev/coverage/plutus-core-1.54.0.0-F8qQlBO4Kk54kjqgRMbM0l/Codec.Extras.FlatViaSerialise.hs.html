    9 {- Note [Flat serialisation for strict and lazy bytestrings]
   10 The `flat` serialisation of a bytestring consists of a sequence of chunks, with each chunk preceded
   11 by a single byte saying how long it is.  The end of a serialised bytestring is marked by a
   12 zero-length chunk.  In the Plutus Core specification we recommend that all bytestrings should be
   13 serialised in a canonical way as a sequence of zero or more 255-byte chunks followed by an optional
   14 final chunk of length less than 255 followed by a zero-length chunk (ie, a 0x00 byte). We do allow
   15 the decoder to accept non-canonical encodings.  The `flat` library always encodes strict Haskell
   16 bytestrings in this way, but lazy bytestrings, which are essentially lists of strict bytestrings,
   17 may be encoded non-canonically since it's more efficient just to emit a short chunk as is.  The
   18 Plutus Core `bytestring` type is strict so bytestring values are always encoded canonically.
   19 However, we serialise `Data` objects (and perhaps objects of other types as well) by encoding them
   20 to CBOR and then flat-serialising the resulting bytestring; but the `serialise` method from
   21 `Codec.Serialise` produces lazy bytestrings and if we were to serialise them directly then we could
   22 end up with non-canonical encodings, which would mean that identical `Data` objects might be
   23 serialised into different bytestrings.  To avoid this we convert the output of `serialise` into a
   24 strict bytestring before flat-encoding it.  This may lead to a small loss of efficiency during
   25 encoding, but this doesn't matter because we only ever do flat serialisation off the chain.  We can
   26 convert `Data` objects to bytestrings on the chain using the `serialiseData` builtin, but this
   27 performs CBOR serialisation and the result is always in a canonical form. -}

   29 -- | For deriving 'Flat' instances via 'Serialize'.
   30 newtype FlatViaSerialise a = FlatViaSerialise { unFlatViaSerialise :: a }
   31 
   32 instance Serialise a => Flat (FlatViaSerialise a) where
   33     -- See Note [Flat serialisation for strict and lazy bytestrings]
   34     encode = encode . BSL.toStrict . serialise . unFlatViaSerialise
   35     decode = do
   36         errOrX <- deserialiseOrFail <$> decode
   37         case errOrX of
   38             Left err -> fail $ show err  -- Here we embed a 'Serialise' error into a 'Flat' one.
   39             Right x  -> pure $ FlatViaSerialise x
   40     size = size . BSL.toStrict . serialise . unFlatViaSerialise
